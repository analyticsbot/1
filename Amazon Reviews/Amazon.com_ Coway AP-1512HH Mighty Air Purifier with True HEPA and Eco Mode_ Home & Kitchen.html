5.0 out of 5 starsBest Air Purifier: Updated 5/16
By Gandalf TOP 1000 REVIEWER on August 10, 2015
Color: Black Verified Purchase
It's a bit bigger than I expected, but by far the most efficient and quiet air purifier I've ever owned.

I found one on Amazon Warehouse deals so I could avoid paying full price. It came with the filters still in their plastic (they're inside the unit).

FAN: The machine has 5 settings, with ionizer as an option for each. There's a low, medium, and high setting, with low being the whisper quiet mode putting out a light breeze, medium being a white noise sounding mode putting out the equivalent to your car fan on max, and high being an audible fan sound, comparable to the sound of your refrigerator running, and puts out an airflow equivalent to sticking your head out of the car window going 30 mph. Then there's the eco setting and auto setting. Both eco and auto are almost the same, putting the fan speed based upon the air quality detected. If the air is good, it switches to low fan speed, medium for moderate quality, and high for poor air quality. Each adjusts the fan speed as the air quality improves or worsens. The difference is on eco setting, if the air quality is good for 30 minutes straight, it will shut off the unit until it detects a change in air quality, whereas auto setting will keep the purifier running, even if just on low fan speed.

IONIZER: The ionizer actually works! It's a setting for any fan speed where it negatively charges the air leaving your fan. Some say that negatively charged air is better for your health and lungs, but I can't prove that so I'll only say what I know for fact. Fact is, in using it for two weeks with the ionizer off, then two weeks with it on, there was a significantly larger amount of dust settled on surfaces in the house.Read more ›

4.0 out of 5 starsA fine air purifier for the price
By Trevor Burnham VINE VOICE on October 11, 2014
Color: Black Vine Customer Review of Free Product ( What's this? )
For the last 3 years, I've owned a RabbitAir MinusA2, and it's dramatically improved the air quality in my apartment, all but eliminating the old building's musty odor. I received the Coway air purifier to review, so I'll attempt to compare the two. I don't have any basis for measuring performance, so I'll focus on features and aesthetics.

The Coway features 3 layers of filters: a washable pre-filter (for dust and hair), a carbon odor filter, and a HEPA filter for tiny particles. That's a good, minimal design for high-quality air filtration. The RabbitAir has all of those plus two more: a "medium" filter between the pre-filter and the HEPA, and a "custom" filter tailored toward whatever type of microparticle you're most worried about. I'd say that anything beyond HEPA + carbon filtration is overkill, but those with certain allergies may disagree. If that's you, take a close look at RabbitAir's custom filters.

Both the Coway and the RabbitAir can automatically adjust their fan speed, from near-silent to about 50dB (roughly the volume level of a normal conversation), based on air quality measurements. This feature could be very annoying if it were implemented poorly, but both devices actually work quite well. Both stay quiet enough that I can keep one in my bedroom and never be woken up by fan noise. Both stay at near-silent level most of the time, only kicking into overdrive when I'm cooking (because of the smoke) or vacuuming (because of the dust). I'm impressed at the technology involved here. I should mention that the RabbitAir can also be adjusted with a remote control (the Coway doesn't have one), but I never use it.

The only clear win for the RabbitAir is it looks.Read more ›

5.0 out of 5 starsGreat air scrubber, with one minor (and one not so minor) flaw
By Rus on March 27, 2016
Color: White Verified Purchase
It's good at what it does. Manage your expectations in terms of room size, and you'll be fine. Truly purifies odors out of the air, and gives it a 'crisp' feel. Is very quiet, on speeds 1 & 2, speed 3 can double as a white noise generator, as I like to sleep like this.

I only have two gripes: the buttons to reset the filter timers are too easy to access, you'll likely press them by accident.

My other gripe is that somehow the speed logic gets wonky on my Coway: I'll set it to speed three, and after some time there is a chance the Coway loses its mind and makes the motor spin a heck of a lot faster than speed 3. This also occurs when it is in auto mode and decides the air is of poor quality (goes to speed 3... then there's a chance it goes to a hidden 'speed 4'). Very odd bug, and it frankly concerns me that it can 'decide' to go way past its limit, as that undoubtedly puts strain past the motor's rating, and could potentially be a fire hazard. Just a note to the mfg., if they're reading this. As such, when I'm away from home, I keep it on 'speed 1', not on 'auto'.

***Update 03/29/16: my apologies to the manufacturer. The 'replace filter' light came on, so I started reading the manual for the first time. I start disassembling the unit (very easy by the way) to access the filters and... discover that both the odor filter and the HEPA filter were wrapped in plastic this whole time. In other words, my gripe about the unit behaving strange was probably my fault, particularly if the motor overdrew current because of heavily restricted airflow. Also, my assessment of the air being 'cleaner' was purely a placebo effect, or maybe just an effect of the pre-filter. So BEFORE USING THIS purifier, remove the filters, and remove their packaging.Read more ›
